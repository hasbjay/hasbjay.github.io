BOOK 1

(Lessons, Debts, and Gratitudes)

Lesson 1

From my first Qur'anic teacher,
that I never meddle with the affairs of others,
and that I never speak evil or ill of anyone.

Lesson 2

From my personal expereince and that of others,
that I shouldn't be satisfied with just the gist of important topics,
but that I dig more into them for better understanding.

Lesson 3

From my observations of my father,
that personal life should be kept private, and what's meant
to be public shold not be kept from the public
nor alter in any shape or from.
And that I be modest in my actions.

Lesson 4

I'm grateful to God that He made me the way he did,
and that He granted me certain traits that He grants only to a few.
One of the traits I'm especially thankful for is my urgent sense of fear of ridiculing others,
for I don't have the courage to look down on even an army of ants.

Lesson 5
To God, for the kind of parents he gave me to,
and that they always do their best to not only provide for my siblings and I,
but also that they educate us by both words and examples.
